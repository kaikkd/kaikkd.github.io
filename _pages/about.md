Hello! I am **Jun Wang (Kaik D.)**, an incoming **M.S. in Computer Science** student at the
**University of California, San Diego (UCSD)** for Fall 2026.

My research centers on **clustering algorithms** and **gravitational optimization**, with a
broader interest in machine learning systems that organize complex real-world data. I am
co-authoring **GCAO: Group-driven Clustering via Gravitational Attraction and Optimization**, a
framework that introduces physics-inspired group dynamics into clustering.

Beyond research, I build and evaluate practical AI systems. At **ByteDance**, I work on
coding-agent engineering and automated evaluation pipelines, including benchmark construction and
real-time agent interfaces.

Outside work, I enjoy a "sea and sunshine" life: surfing, paragliding, and travel. I am always
happy to discuss ML research, AI agents, and good surf spots in San Diego.

See my [publications](/publications/) and [CV](/cv/) for more details.

> "Organizing the world's data through the lens of physics."
